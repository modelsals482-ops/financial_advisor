# Business & Financial Analysis Guide

## What You're Really Asking About

This breaks into 3 distinct problems:

1. **Firm/product analysis** — understanding your own business
2. **Client selection** — figuring out who to target
3. **Approach per client** — how to pitch/serve different segments

---

## 1. Understand Your Own Firm First

Before picking clients, you need clarity on:
- What do you actually sell, and what makes it better than alternatives?
- Who are you already winning with, and why?
- What does it cost you to serve a client vs. what you earn?

**Frameworks to study:**
- **Value Proposition Canvas** (Osterwalder) — maps your product to customer pain/gain
- **Business Model Canvas** — full picture of how your firm operates
- **Unit Economics** — CAC (customer acquisition cost) vs. LTV (lifetime value)

---

## 2. Client Selection (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Frameworks:**
- **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** — define firmographics: industry, size, budget, problem they have
- **TAM / SAM / SOM** — Total addressable market → your realistic slice
- **RFM Analysis** — if you have existing clients: Recency, Frequency, Monetary value to rank them
- **Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)** — what "job" does your client hire your product to do?

**Practical starting point:** Look at your top 3–5 existing clients. What do they have in common? That's your ICP.

---

## 3. Different Approaches for Different Clients

This is essentially **market segmentation**:
- **By size** — SMB vs. mid-market vs. enterprise need completely different sales motions
- **By industry** — same product, different language and pain points
- **By maturity** — early-stage clients need education; mature ones need ROI proof

**Frameworks:**
- **Ansoff Matrix** — grow with existing clients or new ones, existing products or new ones
- **Porter's Five Forces** — understand competitive pressure in each segment
- **Sales qualification: MEDDIC or BANT** — Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline

---

## 4. Competitive Analysis

- **Competitor mapping** — direct vs. indirect competitors, feature/price comparison
- **Positioning map** — plot competitors on 2 axes (e.g., price vs. quality)
- **Win/loss analysis** — why do you win deals? Why do you lose them?

---

## Suggested Study Materials

**Books (in order of priority):**
1. *The Mom Test* — Rob Fitzpatrick (how to talk to clients and understand real needs)
2. *Crossing the Chasm* — Geoffrey Moore (targeting the right customer segments)
3. *Playing to Win* — Roger Martin (strategy and where to compete)
4. *Predictable Revenue* — Aaron Ross (client acquisition systems)
5. *Good Strategy / Bad Strategy* — Richard Rumelt (firm-level strategic clarity)

**Free resources:**
- Harvard Business Review articles on segmentation and ICP
- Y Combinator's startup school videos (practical, even for non-startups)
- Search "unit economics explained" and "ideal customer profile template" on YouTube

---

## Where to Actually Start

1. Write down: who are your current best clients and why
2. Write down: what problem you solve and for whom
3. Share that in chat — Claude can help you build an ICP, analyse your positioning, or stress-test your assumptions
